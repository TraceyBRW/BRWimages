SLEEP
BETTER.
Your most powerful recovery tool.
Sleep isn't just rest — it's the answer to deep recovery. While you sleep, your brain resets, your body repairs, and your emotions process. Hospitality schedules make quality sleep harder — but small changes to how you wind down can make a significant difference to how you feel and perform.
How Sleep Works — The Three Stages & What You Lose Without Them
All night long
LIGHT SLEEP
  • Heart rate & temperature drop
  • Brain begins filtering the day
  • Mental recovery starts
⚠ If missed: foggy & unfocused during your next shift
Hours 1 – 4
DEEP SLEEP
  • Body does heavy repair work
  • Muscles recover from the shift
  • Immune system strengthens
  • Nervous system resets
⚠ If missed: heavy body & lingering soreness
Longer at hours 6 – 8
REM SLEEP
  • Emotions & stress processed
  • Memories organised
  • Mood regulation happens
  • Mental recovery & learning
⚠ If missed: irritability & low stress tolerance

BED = SLEEP ONLY
No scrolling. No TV. No replaying the shift. When you repeatedly do other things in bed, your brain links bed with wakefulness. Protect that association — it matters more than you think.
THE 20-MINUTE RULE
If you're awake and frustrated after about 20 minutes — get out of bed. Go somewhere dim. Do something boring (not your phone). Return only when sleepy. This retrains your brain for better sleep.
