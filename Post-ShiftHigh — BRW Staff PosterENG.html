It's Biology.
During service, your body activates adrenaline, sharpens your senses, and keeps you performing. When your shift ends, this powerful system doesn't just switch off. It needs time and gentle signals to settle. Small choices make big impacts.
How Your Stress System Works — The Three Stages
Stage 01
ALARM
Adrenaline rises. Heart rate up. You're alert, sharp, and in full performance mode.
Great for work
→
Stage 02
RESISTANCE
Still functioning — but under strain. Tired but wired. Many people stay here for weeks.
Burnout begins here
→
Stage 03
EXHAUSTION
The system can no longer sustain prolonged stress. Performance and recovery both suffer.
A Simple Framework for After Your Shift — The 3 A's
Step 1
AWARENESS
Notice your body. Are your shoulders tight? Is your mind still replaying the shift?
Step 2
ASSESSMENT
Ask yourself: Am I still in Alarm mode — or am I stuck in Resistance? Just notice, don't judge.
Step 3
ACTION
Choose one small action that signals safety to your body. That's enough to begin shifting.
